Artist Statements

Project 1: Old Language - New Image

This project, titled Past, Present, and Future, depicts three images that describe a personal journey of self-discovery. When I wanted the viewer to still be able to read the surrounding text, I used lighter colors, such as pink and blue. However, I used more black when that was less important. Having not many materials at my disposal, I made due with pens of various colors, and cut and rearranged the page. The rearranging of pages was inspired by a classmate, Victoria Messinger. The last image was partially inspired by M.C. Escher and his use of negative space.

Project 2: Quietest Place

My project, titled Respite, is a short video of the location I chose as my “quietest place.” The goal of this project was to sit in a relaxing environment and create music inspired by the surroundings. My aim was to create a work that embodied a time of rest in between the hustle and bustle of life. I used a portable USB piano, Ableton Live, and Adobe Premier. Once I found a spot, I got an idea for the music I wanted to make, then filmed and performed the work. Afterward, I edited it all together. The music was inspired by Levi Niha and FKJ.

Project 3: Intimate, Indispensable GIF

The Light in the Dark is a stop-motion GIF that depicts dice for the game of Dungeons & Dragons. Playing D&D with my friends over Zoom during the pandemic allowed me to remain sane during a very stressful time. I wanted a minimalistic aesthetic, I chose dice with neutral colors, and I tried to give the heart in keeping with that theme. I used an app on my phone to take the pictures of all the frames, then Adobe Premier and GIMP to create the video itself, and finally I used Ezgif.com to convert it into a GIF.

Project 4: Word as Image: Kinetic Typography

Unity tells the story of vowels and consonants fighting each other. "Y" breaks up the fight and reminds the other letters that they are fundamentally the same. I picked colors that would make the letters seem angry. I did my best to make use of the 12 principles of animation and make the movement as natural as possible. I used After Effects to animate, Ableton Live to create the "musical" sound effects, and Premier Pro to stitch the two together. The story hinges on the sound, which starts as an arrhythmic jumble, and ends with a song, emphasizing the coordination in the movement of the individual characters.

Project 5: Animated Conversation

This animation, titled Comics, brings to life a conversation between myself and my partner. It attempts to visually show what my partner is talking about. I chose a relaxing, cool color scheme to create a pleasant looking jungle river. I was able to use some of my experience with the last project to more easily implement the 12 fundamental principles of animation. I used Illustrator to create the graphics, Animate to create the animation, and Premier Pro to add the sound effects. I wanted the sound to accompany the video rather than distract from it, so I made the sounds relatively quiet.
